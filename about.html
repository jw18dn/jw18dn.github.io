Motivation and Biography

About Me

I am a scientist that works on improving the abilities of legged robots. My passion is to take on the most interesting and complex problems necessary for building and controlling electromechanical systems. Although my degree is in mechanical engineering, the majority of my work involves developing control algorithms for robots and programming in C++ and/or python. Since leaving academia my focus has been on making these systems work in the real world.

Professional Biography

Dr. Jason White received his Ph.D. from Florida State University, where his dissertation focused on "Real-Time Planning and Control of Dynamic Robots in Dynamic Environments." His research spans model-predictive control, dynamic obstacle avoidance, and force-based control of bipedal robots.

During his time at Florida State University, he worked in the Adaptive and Reactive Robotics Lab under Dr. Christian Hubicki. His work has been published in top robotics venues including ICRA, IROS, and Humanoids. His contributions include novel approaches to real-time motion planning using quadratic programming, online gait optimization in resistive media, and through-contact locomotion generation via model-predictive control.
